My Australian project

1. Where is Australia?
Australia is in the Southern Hemisphere, to the East of the Indian Ocean.

2. What are Australia's five biggest cities?
They are all on the coast:
- Sydney: 4,840,628 people
- Melbourne: 4,440,328 people
- Brisbane: 2,274,460 people
- Perth: 2,021,203 people
- Adelaide: 1,304,631 people

3. What is a marsupial?
Marsupials are a group of mammals that are known for carrying their babies in a pouch.

4. Name three marsupials that live in Australia.
Three marsupials that live in Australia are kangaroos, koalas and wombats.

5. Name five dangerous animals that live in Australia. How dangerous are they? Where can they be found?
- The box jellyfish has venom on its tentacles and can be found on most beaches from October to May.
- The inland taipan snake has enough venom to kill 100 men. It is found below ground in the outback.
- The eastern brown snake has venom that stops blood clotting. It is found in populated areas.
- Honey bees give many people allergic reactions. They are everywhere!
- Blue ringed octopus has enough poison to kill 26 people. They are found in many places including Sydney Harbour.

6. What is the Great Barrier Reef? Where is it?
The Great Barrier Reef is the world's largest coral reef. It is in Queensland in Australia.

7. What is damaging the reef and how?
The crown thorn starfish is the biggest threat to the Great Barrier Reef. It sticks on the coral and poisons the coral.

8. What is the weather like in Australia?
Mostly sunny and warm.

9. Which seasons are in which months? How is this different from the UK and why?
When it is Summer in Australia, it is winter in the UK. In December it is cold and snowy in the UK but in Australia it is sunny. Imagine having Christmas on the beach!

10. Which sports are popular in Australia?
Three popular sports in Australia are cricket, football and swimming.

11. Name two famous sights in Sydney? When were they built?
Two of the most famous sights in Sydney are the Harbour Bridge, built in 1932, and the Sydney General Post Office, built in 1891.

12. What are your top five things to do in Sydney and why?
My five favourite things in Sydney are:
- Swimming, because there were crabs in the outside pool on Bronte beach.
- Surfing on Palm Beach because I had never done it before.
- Getting around by ferry because you get to see the whole city.
- Climbing the Harbour Bridge - my Daddy did it and it sounded amazing. I want to do it when I am older.
- Visiting the Botanic Gardens where we fed the cockatoos and found Australian conkers.
