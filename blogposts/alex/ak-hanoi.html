Hello Hanoi

We are in Hanoi, the capital of Vietnam. In Vietnam, they speak Vietnamese and they have a lot of temples. In one temple there were 278 statues. Wow, that’s a lot. The buddhas have big tummies and big ears that go down to their shoulders so that they can hear everything.

We went to the mausoleum for Ho Chi Minh. We were not allowed to talk when we were looking at him. But I thought the guards were statues because they were standing very still.

Then we saw a one-pillar-pagoda and I thought it was very weird because the buddha on the altar has 12 hands.

The traffic is very busy. And sometimes we have to walk on the edge of the road because the pavements are crammed with scooters.

The buildings are very narrow. At the bottom of all buildings there is a shop. Behind the shop there is the house.

We went for a rickshaw ride around Hanoi. They are like bicycles but with a sofa in the front.

We also went to the water puppets. The wooden puppets were actually in the water. The 4 sacred animals are: the turtle (long life and hard work), the dragon (strength), the phoenix (prosperity) and the unicorn (protection).
