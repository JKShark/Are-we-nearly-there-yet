Ascending dragons

The Mekong river starts in China and meets the sea in Vietnam. We went to the bit by the sea, called the Mekong Delta. The Vietnamese call it the land of the ascending dragons.

The people live in houses on stilts over the water. If the stilts broke, their houses would fall into the water. Everyone has a boat. No one has a car. They get around on boats. The river is like a road for them.

On our first boat trip, we saw people washing their hair in the river and washing their clothes in the river. The river had bits of floating grass and leaves in it. It was very cloudy. I would not like to wash my clothes in the river.

Then we went into a little stream off the main river. We went under some bridges. On one of the bridges, we saw some boys jumping off the bridge into the river. We got splashed when one of the boys jumped in right next to us. They looked like they were having a lot of fun. The river was like a playground for them.

We also saw a floating market. You know what someone wants to sell because they put what they are selling on a bamboo stick high in the air on their boat.

At the end of the day, I wasn’t hungry because we went to a factory with coconut sweets and popped rice and dried banana and peanut butter flavoured toffee. I loved that part!
