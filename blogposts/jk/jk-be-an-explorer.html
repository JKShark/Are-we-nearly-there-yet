Be an explorer

The future is an excuse to do nothing in the present. We'll go travelling next year. When Amy has finished her exams. When my promotion comes through. When we've saved up just a bit more money. But I don’t think there’s time to waste. Time is our only real commodity. Nothing else matters. Go looking for adventure in real life. Be bold. Make decisions. Be an explorer.

Extended travel is unusual these days but not an unusual concept. Nor is it particularly trendy or modern. But, having had the opportunity to go on this trip, I’m beginning to think it’s essential.

Extended travel removes you from daily life. Increases your options not your personal possessions. Gives you time to think. Away from the routine. Away from family and friends. Allows you to be yourself or reinvent yourself. No need to fit into familial roles or play your usual character. No peer pressure. No benchmarks. No normal. Just you and your loved ones. And if you’re putting on a act in front of them, you’ll soon be found out.

Freedom is to have a wealth of time. We’ve no plans for today. How rare is that? We’re free.

So obviously I find myself in MacDonald’s.

Having freedom and choices still means sometimes you end up at the golden arches. Yes, we’ve landed on another “Yes” day. Somehow the kids have been good enough to build up the credit to take control. So I’ve been in toys shops and clothes shops while the world outside enjoys the autumn sun. People without my freedom go sailing in the Sydney harbour and marvel at my lack of plans, my abundance and my fast food meal in a windowless food court.

But there is still joy to be found. The kids revel in our powerlessness and are surprisingly restrained. They are loving this. And who am I to argue? We still steer the choices down known cul-de-sacs. And it’s fun. I even manage to sneak into the garden and sit down to write this in the afternoon sun - so it’s not all bad.

I’ve started reading a book called Vagabonding which attempts to explain the need for extended travel. It is preaching to the converted. If I'd read it a year ago, I’d have quit my job and set off on a round the world trip. Good job I only discovered it today. But you should read it.

Embrace the uncertainty. Change your mindset. Stop making excuses. Start looking at maps and feel the pull of endless possibility.
