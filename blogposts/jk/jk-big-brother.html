Big Brother's lazy

They're watching you. Peeking over your shoulder. Checking every tip-tap on your phone. Cameras trained on you like models on a shoot. Software being tweaked to recognise you. Big Brother is out there.

But he just needs to read this. Or check out my Instagram account. That'll tell you where I am. No need to test your processing power on me. But I was reading up about privacy and online security and it got me scared. Paranoid. So I stayed up late (9pm) downloading some cool software that stops malware and hides my location. I wanted to test it to the max.

So I turn up at Sydney airport in hat, scarf and silly beard (actually that's ever present at the moment). Turned my phone onto stealth mode. First I connect to the airport wifi (though it thinks I'm in Melbourne - ha!). I have over 1,000 tracking attempts blocked within 5 minutes. Wow. No doubt advertisers wanting to sell me. The hat and scarf make me out to be a weirdo immediately. It's 20 degrees. I'm asked to take them off. Bugger. They get a good look at my face.

They have to scan my passport to let me get airside. There's no pretending I'm not there now. They know. My passport tells them my nationality, my date of birth, my place of birth. No doubt it also has tracks of where we've been and when it was last scanned (when we entered the country).

Then they give me a little form to fill out. In pen. Even ones for the kids too. Old school espionage this. I have to copy the information out from my passport onto this little bit of paper. Put my occupation down too. That's a tricky one. Not sure what to write. So I put ‘housewife'. That'll fool them. They want to know the kids' occupation too. Weird. Haven’t they've just scanned our passports?

But too soon, we're on the plane. And no sooner have we taken off than I'm asked to fill out some more little bits of paper. Again I copy out the details from my passport. And answer trick questions like, “Are you a terrorist?" I hope I tick the right box.

We've been box ticking our way around the world. I can't help thinking it's pointless. In this world which knows where I am at all times. Where privacy has gone. What use is an extra little bit of paper with duplicate information? I'd really like to know what’s done with it. And how it helps them catch someone they want to catch?

Maybe I'm just frustrated at having to use a pen at all. It's been a while now. And it hurts my fingers. I grip it too tight. Anxious not to get the trick questions wrong. (Really though, what is my occupation? “None" sounds wrong, even though it's right. And picks me out as an anomaly. You're unemployed? But you're here for a month. What are you going to do? Nothing? I don't believe you. You're bringing in some mud, alfalfa seeds and $10,000. You're going to try to stay forever and be a farmer aren't you, boy?)

Maybe they don't want to admit how much they know about you. So they ask you to tell them all the boring stuff they already know. Just to make you feel better about the horrific invasion of privacy and borderline stalking that really goes on. I'd rather they just admit it.

Big Brother is here to stay. And he sure ain't lazy.
