Big thoughts

Amazingly, I still need to slow down. To enjoy the pace. Not to hurry. I’m sure my current life is glacial compared to back home but we are still busy. I want some time to sit and stare at fluffy clouds floating by and think some big thoughts.

Yeah, yeah, yeah. I know what you're thinking. I've gone all hippy now that I've hit Bali. But I don't want to change the world. I’m fairly sure that won’t happen with my feet dangling in a swimming pool. But I do want to spend a bit more time thinking. Not reading. Not sleeping. Not playing hide and seek with the kids (as an aside, in hotel rooms, they are always in the cupboard. There’s nowhere else to hide. And if they are two years old, then they always hide in the same place, even after you’ve just found them. Try it. It’s funny the first time and then you end up lying on the bed shouting out, "You’re in the cupboard again."). Just alone with my thoughts. I’ve no idea what these will be but I’d like to find out.

I’ve never been very good at introspection. The past is the past. No point reflecting on that. But it would be good to know a bit more about myself. What makes me tick. If I don’t know, then nobody else does (not that anyone else cares of course, and why would they?). What do I want from life? What do I want to do?

As much as I don’t want to try to plan for the future, it would be good to come up with some themes to follow. Some guidelines for life. At least for the rest of life. Maybe I could even turn these into some helpful advice for my kids. You always read about kids who've picked up some key sound bites from their dad. You know the kind of thing: “Work hard and you’ll achieve whatever you want”; “Never go to sleep at a party with a block of stilton for a pillow”; “Never get drunk and wet the bed with your girlfriend asleep next to you”. OK, that last one is something I picked up from a friend at college. Good advice though.

I’m clearly not alone with these thoughts. The CFO of Google has just retired aged 50-odd to travel and spend time with his kids. So I’m in good company. I do think the nature of work is changing. Technology and the endless pursuit of growth is changing the landscape. Work and careers will not be the same for our kids. I’m not yet sure how to prepare them for this future. But experiencing life, embracing change and being flexible are all going to be important.

I hope they pick up something from this trip. Even if it’s only how to cope with change, and how silly their father looks with any facial hair whatsoever.
