Catch me

Playing 'it' is probably my kids' favourite game. Behind watching TV, playing on iPhones and watching TV, that is.

Running around trying to catch each other is a lot of fun, it seems. But when nations start to play, things get serious. India and China are vast countries with vast populations and they are doing their best to play catch with the West.

But at what cost? I'm no green apologist (as my friends, giggling at the back, know only too well) but the smog here in China is killing me. We've been to the North, the centre and now the South and the background has always been the same. Grey fog. Everwhere we look. Some children in China have never seen blue sky. Ever! The level of pollution in Beijing when we were there was 20 times the safe limit.

It's going to be a huge problem. Healthcare costs will surely impact future growth. According to the World Health Organization, the number of cancer patients and deaths because of lung and stomach disease accounted for 30 and 40 percent of all cancer cases. Air and water pollution is the main suspected cause.

Companies such as Coke are now paying people to go and work in China. A sort of smog danger money. I wouldn't gamble with my health for some extra dosh. But it's happening.

I have no idea what can be done. But something must change. For once the evidence of destruction has burst my apathy. The people we met felt the same.

Surely a billion people can change the world?
