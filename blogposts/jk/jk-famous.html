When will I, will I be famous?

There are no holidays in China. You can only take holiday when the government tells you. That makes Chinese New Year the biggest holiday on the planet - a billion people have a week off. All at the same time.

The run up to New Year was quiet as people were making their preparations for a traditional, family New Year's Eve celebration. Since then it has been apocalyptically busy. We've been doing the tourist thing, of course. And so has the rest of China.

We arrived in Xi'an to see the Terracotta Warriors, along with a few million locals. Most of them from the 'countryside'. A word that conjures up images of peaceful farming types, but appears to be used as a slang term in the same way posh Londoners talk about anyone not from London. None of them spoke a word of a English and none of them had ever seen a Westerner before.

We had so many pictures taken of our children that you'd think they were the tourist attraction, not the 2,000 year old clay army. If all the photos had been uploaded to Facebook at the same time, we'd have crashed Facebook. Except of course that it's banned. Some wiry old granny even wanted a photo with me. Well, why wouldn't she?

Today is our first day in Shanghai and whilst it's a very different place, the crowds have a similar view of our children. If we stop too long in any one place, a circle of cameras begins to form. Then gets larger. Then people start grabbing one or more of our children to get a photo with them. Before long it starts to feel out of control. Like we may lose a child. Kind of crazy. Though to be honest, that's one of the good things about having three kids. Losing one doesn't seem so bad. Hey, we'd still have two.

So we don't stand still. We keep moving. We don't let the crowds form. Like a shark circling our tourist attraction prey. All our photos are blurred as a result - they're taken on the hoof.

If this is a glimpse into being famous, having the paparazzi hound you, then it's rubbish. Though I guess for me it's more of a glimpse into being the bodyguard of someone famous. I’m in every photo. At the edge. The big bloke next to the famous bloke. Nobody knows who I am.

I can't wait for everyone to go back to work.
