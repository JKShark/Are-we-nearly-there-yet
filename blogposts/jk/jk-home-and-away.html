Home and away

5 beaches in 5 days. Bronte, Tamarama, Palm, Bondi and Manly. Surfing. Barbies. Sailing. Sydney. This is it.

This is Autumn. But we've spent every day outside. All day. With shades and sunscreen. This wasn't meant to be a beach holiday. This was our time to be settled. Nestled in our four bedroom rental house in the suburbs. A bit like home. And we’re very comfortable here. But the pull of the outdoors is strong here. And we can't resist.

Running along the coastal path. Football in the garden. Getting around by boat instead of car. And the beach.

I'm not normally much of a beach person. Too much sand. Too much hassle. Give me a pool any day. But the beaches here are something else. Enormous. Clean. Waves for entertainment. And beautiful. You could spend weeks just photographing them. We’ve chosen to enjoy them, with added surf.

We’ve now actually been surfing. All of us except Helen and Freddie. And we love it. It's Madeleine's favourite new sport now (obvs). I found it hard. Very hard. And after an hour I was so sore that I couldn't breathe without pain. Two days later and my ribs feel like I've taken up boxing not surfing. I'm prone to exaggeration but this is no joke. I couldn't bend down to take off my thongs (and no I haven’t altered my underwear habit). I couldn't lie down without pain. I'd done something to myself. Something bad. I’ve attempted to defy middle age.

A forty something newbie is a rare thing. Or maybe I'm a grommet. I'm not sure. There’s a whole lexicon that's beyond me. I'm not a dude apparently. I'm not goofy. I don't know what it's like be be kegged but I have been hammered (in more ways than one). And I do know that surfing is insane.

Our chosen location was Palm Beach (Palmie). A nice easy intro with no wind and small waves. Shane was our instructor. Of course he was. When I say instructor, he was more of a companion. There wasn’t much instruction. Not enough as it turns out. I’ve since learnt more from Google than Shane. But at least I’ve experienced what it’s like to be wave powered. It is an amazing feeling. Surprising somehow that something so simple can be so much fun. But boy it’s hard. I have a feeling, like most things, it’d be easier if I was a tad lighter of body. No matter. I can’t get out of bed without pain so there’s no chance of me having another go yet.

With my pink board and my lack of balance, the real grommies viewed me with a mixture of awe and derision. This is a young man's game. You need to be fit and flexible. But I'll be back. When my body lets me.

Which may be a while.
