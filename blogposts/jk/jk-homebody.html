A Homebody Abroad

It occurred to me today that I like it at home. All my things. My own bed. Everything in its place. The premier league. Creature comforts. I always have preferred to stay home rather than go out (just ask my sister).

So why have I left? Giving up all of that for 8 months on the road. Living out of suitcases. New places. New cultures. New languages. No friends. Or at least new transient friendships. Not like the ones at home.

I'm not sure why. I seem to have a strong desire for adventure and for new experiences at the moment. In the last two years I've completed an Ironman, left my job, become a Mamil, learnt to code, and now left behind my home comforts to take the family travelling.

Maybe this is my mid life crisis? It's hardly going off the rails. A desire to spend more time with my family doesn't feel like a crisis to me. But there's definitely a desire for change. And I guess that's the usual driver behind middle aged men (usually) buying fast cars and trying to relive their youth.

So it will be interesting to see how this homebody copes with extended travel. I've been worrying about the kids being homesick. Maybe it's me I should be worried about.
