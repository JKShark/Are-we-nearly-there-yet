How to lose a child

Go to a large populated place such as, say, China. Assume your children have some common sense. Then take your eyes off of them for about 5 seconds. And three magically becomes two.

Then panic.

And then calm down. We can't have lost him. He was here just a second ago. Then realise that he really isn't here and start to panic again.

And then run aimlessly shouting Alex in increasingly desperate screams.

So we were just out shopping. We needed a new suitcase because the careful handling of airline ‘luggage services’ had ripped a hole in one of ours and we were just talking about how enormous the Chinese shopping malls were, when we lost Alex.

I know that I've joked about how having three children means you won't miss losing one of them. Turns out you do mind. Quite a lot actually.

I ran out of the shop shouting Alex. A hundred yards gone. No sign of him. I reached a T-junction. Left or right? My future happiness depended on getting this call right. Perhaps my marriage. Definitely the mental health of my other two children. Never has a 50:50 chance call been so important.

I shout Alex really very loudly. Get lots of stares from the locals. A man helpfully uses sign language to ask whether what I’m searching for is about one metre high. He is. The man points left and I run off shouting Alex with every breath.

I’m now breathing hard and sweating like a, well, like a parent who's lost his child.

Two hundred yards later, I spot him. Thank God. That was the longest 300 yards of my life. He was wandering aimlessly, on his own. I pick him up and half crush him to death.

He thought we’d left the shop without him and he was off to find us. Why would we ever leave without him? Where was he was going? How would he ever have found us? And what would have happened if we hadn’t noticed he’d left the shop when we did? Who knows? It was close.

Neither Alex nor I quite got to the stage of full on panic. It felt like a warning. A brush with disaster. I'm not sure Alex has learned much from the experience but I certainly have. The main lesson? Never assume your children have any common sense at all. It’s beginning to seem like the main purpose of parenting may be to teach them common sense. That’s it. That’s all.

How hard can that be?
