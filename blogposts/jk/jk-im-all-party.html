I'm all business at the front, party at the back

Like Samson, I'm scared to cut my hair. I want to look like a traveller (or vagrant as my wife puts it). It's part of the experience. I don't want to look business. I'm no suit. I'm all party.

And while I love the locals here in Syndey, I don't want to look like them. My hair is approaching mullet levels. I fit in perfectly. But my family have started to comment. I don't want to be too party.

We escape to the countryside (where my look is even more local). People tell us not to bother. But there's a storm hitting Sydney and it seems like a good time to escape. We head into the blue hills. They calls them mountains but they're not. Unless Box Mountain in Surrey has changed its name.

It's proper countryside here. Cute villages with no apparent reason for existing. National park forever (only 8 million acres). And it's beautiful. Even through the rain. We've found an amazing place to stay. An old farm house used for storing straw. Corrugated iron walls. Open fires. And no heating. We've gone from surfing in Sydney in the sunshine to shivering in the hills in only a few days. It feels like winter. But the storms battering Sydney sound even worse. So we hunker down, light a fire and hug a glass of red. We're fine.

At first light, we're off to hunt kangaroos. With complaints about the time from our kids we bundle them into a 4x4 and head into the valley. It's about 300km long so what do they name it? Megalong Valley (obvs). And it's still chucking it down. We see wombats, wallabies and hundreds of kangaroos. And I mean literally hundreds. We see baby Joeys and big angry Bucks. For Londoners, it’s very cool. They’re such strange beasts.

A quick cup of coffee under the tailgate and we head back, through the jungle and across the overflowing rapids, back to safety. Much more rain and the only road will be impassable. The river already flowing up over the top of the bridge.

Most of the things to do here are outdoors. And the weather doesn't want us out there. We need to be back by the fire. Drying out. We don't have clothes for these conditions. But we're here. Everywhere's deserted. We have a free run.

So we go and see the Three Sisters. Driving through the clouds to get there. A coach load of Chinese beats us. Miraculously the clouds part, the rain stops and we get some awesome photos.

For someone who's all party, it was a pretty chilled couple of days. Back in Sydney to assess the damage, the storm is dying at last. The damage though will take a while to fix. But go to the Blue Mountains. Ignore the naysayers. It's like the Lake District with less lakes. Just Go.
