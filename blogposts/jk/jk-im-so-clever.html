I'm so clever and classless and free

I'm beginning to think that my life to date has all been a warm up. They say that you have only one life to live. But what if you have lots of lives? And you're free to end one and start another at any point?

There's a well known but powerful experiment done by Stanford University with kids in the 1970s. The psychologist sits them down at a table in a room on their own (no parents, no toys, no distractions). A feat worthy of a prize in itself. He puts a single marshmallow on the table in front of them and tells them that he is popping out for 15 minutes. If they can wait, resist the urge to eat the marshmallow, then they can have two when he returns.

Could you resist? What if the marshmallow was your email inbox and it pinged away during those fifteen minutes? Or it was your facebook feed and all you could see was the notification icon slowly ticking up? How many kids do you think were able to wait? Could you?

The clever bit was the follow up. Ten years later, that simple experiment could explain better performance in school, lower BMI and better 'life success' (though try defining that).

It got me thinking - is there nothing that can't be achieved with delayed gratification (and hard work)?

You see, I thought I'd lost my ambition. Achieved enough. Done well enough. Time to kick back and relax. But recently I've come to realise that I still have ambitions. And it'd be an insult to ignore those feelings. I feel like I'm about to start my second life. Or maybe my third or fourth. I'm not entirely clear what it will be. I'm just going to do the work. And start living my unlived life.

I’m no working class hero. But I do feel like there’s change coming. Jobs are more temporary. Things are different now. But what do I know? I’m just a fucking peasant. Just like all the rest.
