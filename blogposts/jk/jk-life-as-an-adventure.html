Life as an adventure

Ok. So I've not yet talked about the elephant in the room.

Her name's Victoria. She’s big and grey as you'd expect. And I'm about to climb onto her back. No really. This isn't a metaphor. There really is an elephant in the room.

We went elephant trekking today. Mainly because Freddie loves elephants and we figured we may never get the chance to do it again. An hour on Victoria’s back, walking slowly through the jungle. And boy, was it hot. No shade, midday sun and a mildly terrified Freddie acting as hot water bottle in my arms.

We did stop for a rest at a watering hole. Victoria (aged 47) and her partner for the day Hero (aged 15, carrying Helen and Madeleine) cooled themselves down and us in the process. I’ve never enjoyed having muddy, cholera infected water sprayed onto me and my camera as much as I did then. Oh, and I got to see an elephant penis. The donkey in the corner who was feeling quietly smug is now hugely embarrassed. Hung like an elephant is the new normal. Easily the highlight of the trip so far.

Our ‘guides’ who, up until that point, had sat atop the animal’s head then (hilariously) decided to dismount and leave us up there to get home on our own.

But it’s OK, they got straight back on...after we'd shit ourselves.

No, they bloody didn’t!

They had a fag and watched as I hung on to Freddie in a death grip with Alex beside me (just chilling’) whilst Victoria ambled off into the jungle.

But it was fine. Eventually. The elephants obviously knew the way home.

I’d like to have known a bit more about these particular elephants and the history of elephants in Thailand but it wasn’t that sort of tour. We did, however, decide that we wanted to take Victoria home with us so she could take the kids to school.

We just need to build her a hutch in the garden first. (Do elephants live in a hutch? Or is that rabbits?)
