I'm lost

I'm wandering around Hoi An, an ancient port on the east coast of Vietnam. And I'm lost.

Of course, I'm not really lost. I've just told you where I am. But with lots of tiny streets with strange names and my infamous sense of direction, I don't know where I am.

And I'm loving it. We have no timetable. No agenda. And I've got Helen to get me back to the hotel.

It's nice to feel lost sometimes. I'm usually on firm ground. Home turf. In London. My home for 20 years. Familiar sights and sounds.

Nothing is familiar here. Strange creatures with black teeth hawking some junk. Unrecognisable shapes in soup bowls masquerading as food.

I feel like a spy. Trying to decipher a code. There are (street) signs. There are (Chinese) symbols. Everyone else knows the cipher.

But I'm lost.
