Mission impossible

Today I ventured out alone into the cold wild land. Surrounded by aliens. All wearing black. With masks concealing their foreign features.

We've been here for two days now and I've cast off my safety net. I haven't left Simone's side for a second since she met us at the airport. It's been too scary.

She’s been amazing. We've seen things we'd never have seen. Done things we'd never have done (like leave the house!) and it's been great to catch up with a good friend after 6 weeks away.

We are, of course, in Japan. The land that economic growth forgot. Familiar yet so different. Vast yet cozy and cute. Simone used to live here and speaks localese fluently. What a different experienced we've had with someone who can chat to the locals, ask directions, navigate the underground (God knows how we are going to manage when she leaves us. We are seriously thinking of kidnapping her.) We have all fallen in love with Japan.

Karaoke, Electric City, Kidzania, Park Hyatt, New York Grill, Tsukiji fish market, Meji shrine, Shibuya crossing. We’ve done a lot and seen a lot. We’ve done the tourist stuff as we’re only here a few days. But it’s been great. Even the mundane is a treat here. The tube. The taxi drivers (who are completely useless. They speak no English (though I don’t blame them for that) but even with the address written down in Japanese, they often fail to be able to use their sat nav. Honestly, without Simone, we would still be wandering around Tokyo saying “I’m sure this looks familiar”, trying to get home).

And so now, after two days of orientation, I feel able to walk the streets alone. I emerge into a cold crisp blue skied morning armed with ridiculously detailed (but necessary) instructions from Simone.

Ten minutes later I arrive at my destination, the easy bit over. I now have to speak to one of these beautiful strangers. I now need to attempt to communicate. I can’t even pretend to utter some words learned long ago. Some teenage language skills, tripping up over gender and conjugation. I have no clue. I can just about say thank you (think of pasta and mumble “A Rigatoni” and it just about works).

So I say as clearly as I can, “Double tall latte, please” and hold my breathe. And she says, “Is that to stay or to go?” Phew! Mission accomplished. Maybe not impossible but certainly difficult.

In this strange and beautiful land, it's me who is the alien.
