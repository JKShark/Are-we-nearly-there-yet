The Panda vs. The Mouse

Today we saw a panda fighting a mouse. And the mouse won.

I'd been determined to throw in a few days just for the kids when planning this trip. Too many temples, too much history and they'd lose enthusiasm. I want them to enjoy this as much as us. However, the last minute addition of Tokyo Disneyland and a trip to Happy Valley Amusment Park today meant two theme parks in five days.

It wasn't a fair fight. It’s off season here in Beijing and the park was more or less empty with most of the main rides closed. The kids still had a ball of course. And I was dragged on enough rides to leave me feeling like I didn't know which way was up.

The one noticeable difference was a level of grumpiness from the staff working in the park that I’ve only seen before on commuters’ faces on the London underground. Disneyland opens here in a couple of years. It will be interesting to see whether they can train the locals to be not just happy and smiling, but Disney happy - a whole other level of happiness. They've done it in Japan (very polite, very smiley). It hasn’t worked too well in Paris (mainly weather related but surely influenced by grumpy insouciant Frenchmen).

We finished the day by going to see the world's largest television (350 metres wide). And why not? A good end to a slightly surreal day of ‘sightseeing’. But how to quench my TV envy?
