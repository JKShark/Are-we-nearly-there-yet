Predicting the future

I wonder how our kids are going to turn out. I'm sure everyone does. I only hope we can help them to grow angel's wings.

You try to treat them the same. You shout at each of them equally. You don't swear. You buy them all presents (a few more for your favourite). You say No as much as anyone has ever said anything ever. And you try to be a role model. Then they grow, live, and learn. They all have the same genes or at least they have pulled from the same well. So how are they so different?

Spending so much time with my kids has let me understand them in a way usually reserved for Mums. Though when I say understand, I mean in the same way that I understand what plants feel.

All this time together and they are still little unexplored planets. Reacting in increasingly bizarre and unpredictable ways. You see their different behaviours. Their different personalities. And try vainly to control them or at least mould them somewhat towards being sensible human beings. I wonder whether you can predict how they'll turn out at this age (8, 6 and 2)?

I hope not. They have so much more living to do. I also wonder how this trip will affect them. Hopefully positively. But who knows. Is getting to know their Dad better a good thing? Maybe Dads are supposed to be absent. To be at work. Is me not working, but being ever present, going to be worse for them than if it was at home working, but never saw them?

We can't do anything about their birth order or their birth date. Both of which have a bigger effect on them than much of what I say or do. But hopefully we can influence them over time. They can absorb some of my many years of wisdom. Won’t take long I hear you all shout.

One clear difference is the way they play. Madeleine has a toy that she has loved since she was probably only 3 years old. Every other toy since then has struggled to compare. She still returns to it even now. Alex loves all toys equally. No favourites. No new ones or old ones - they are all great. And Freddie loves one toy so deeply and passionately that he literally doesn’t put it down. Not for eating, sleeping or whilst on the loo. Then one day, for no reason at all, it will be discarded and a new love replaces the old.

I wonder if their relationships will be the same as they get older? If patterns of attachment have somehow already been set. We’ll see.

If so, I pity Freddie’s future girlfriends.
