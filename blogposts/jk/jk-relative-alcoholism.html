Relative alcoholism

My ongoing battle with the booze took another hit today. My parents arrived.

It’s not like I’m an alcoholic or anything. Though the amount I bang on about it, maybe I’m on track. But I do recognise that it’s not healthy, full of calories and makes my head hurt the next day.

Up until this point we had limited our alcohol to the evenings. Ten minutes after my parents arrived, at lunchtime, the beers were out and a bottle of wine had been opened. And we had a fantastic long lunch. No doubt the wine helped, but the setting is something else. Our villa here in Bali basically has no walls. It’s so hot and dry (most of the time), that walls aren’t needed. So our dining room is a standalone roof on stilts surrounded by blue skies and tropical flowers. And boy is it hot here. Mid 30s every day so far and humidity off the scale. You feel slightly damp all the time. Which might be a problem if you weren’t always in one of three states: in the pool; in air conditioning; or outside with a cold beer in your hand.

I think it would be hard to work here. For a start, everyone slows right down so you don’t get a full on sweat going. Even your brain synapses seem to react slower to every stimulus. And secondly, I’d always be either hungover or drunk. Those are the only two states. If you feel neither, then its time to open a beer. It’s also a good way to tell the time because that means its probably lunch time.

But I don’t care. My clean and lean diet (yeah, right!) can take a hike.

My parents are only here for a week and we're going to have a good time. We are going to hit the shops, the restaurants and the beach. We may even visit a temple. We haven’t seen each other for three months which may be no time at all for some but we all live close together in London (me, sis, the mother in law and my parents all within a tight 2 mile radius) and see each other every week, so its been a while.

And we're catching up. The children are loving it too. Great big squeezy hugs when they got here and much love since. No doubt helped that the grandparents brought sweets and presents with them, even though they had to carry them half way around the world! You’ve got to love them.

And I do. Now hand me that beer.
