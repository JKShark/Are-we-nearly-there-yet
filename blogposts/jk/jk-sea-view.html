Sea view

It feels like flying. Looking down from above. Sea breeze keeping me afloat. The spray on my face reminding me where I am. And the horizon stretching out beyond.

Sydney is an extraordinary place. It's breathtaking. Not in the way that you normally think. The word thrown down instead of using a perfectly good word like beautiful. But as in literally. It's hard to breathe here. The wind whipping the air from your lungs. The views around each and every corner jaw droppingly magical, punching you in the gut. If we physically swapped Europe with Australia, then England would be empty. Everyone would live here. It's only the distance that keeps us apart.

We know people here too. Old friends. And before we'd even unpacked we'd seen two of them - their home now here. And it's strange to see fellow escapees. Theirs more permanent than ours. They were happy. They were comfortable. They didn't need before.

So we did what we wanted. We started living. No mad rush to see the sights. We have time. We haven't even seen the bridge yet or the harbour. We're loving our home here by the beach.

I feel like I'm Columbusing (discovering something for the first time that's been around for centuries). But Sydney has that effect. Nobody comes here and doesn't want to stay. I know my parents are worried I'll fall in love with the place and the lifestyle. It's just so far away from friends and family. But it's not the distance that attracts. For some maybe but not for me.

It's the sea.

It's like Cornwall with the heater on. Shinier and sunnier and warmer. Sitting on the balcony looking over the ocean. The blue expanse. Engulfing my concentration. Distracting me. A home with a view. A beach to go surfing on. A boat to nip into the harbour. A picnic on the water. How can London compete with that?

What is it about a horizon full of ocean that is so magnetic? Do we see so little horizon these days in our cramped tall cities that a view of big sky and horizon stretching from eye to eye takes the breath away. It's a physical feeling too. At least for me. Breathless and emotional.

How about winter in Sydney? Summer in France? If only the children didn't need to go to school. Someone needs to open a school for peripatetic kids. Same syllabus globally. You can pop in and out with a term's notice. Live anywhere. Anyone keen?

Until then we'll keep dreaming. Keep house hunting.
