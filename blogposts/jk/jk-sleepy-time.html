Sleepy time

As parents, sleep is a key topic of conversation. A source of almost constant tension. Who’s getting it? Who isn’t? I’m better at it than you. You just lie there; how hard is that? It’s my turn to get some tonight. And so on.

Most of our friends have forgotten what it’s like. But we have a two year old who needs to sleep every 7 hours or so otherwise, well, it’s not worth seeing what happens if he doesn’t. Which makes most things challenging. Long lunch? Well, maybe. But half way through, we need to find somewhere dark and flat to put our monster out for the count. And getting drunk over a long lunch is also challenging. Honey, do you remember where we put him? Yeah, yeah, somewhere dark and flat, you know? You remember? So we end up carrying a baby buggy everywhere, even though he can walk now, albeit slowly. Just so he can have a sleep if he needs to. You get to choose to carry a heavy unwieldy buggy around hot dusty uneven streets or carry a hot two year old whilst walking through the dust in temperatures more appropriate for roasting the sunday joint.

Oh I know what you’re thinking. We're too precious. He doesn’t need to sleep. Just keep him up. Chill out. Enjoy yourselves. Children are very adaptable these days. And you’re right.

Up to a point.

We are pretty chilled out by now. We don’t care whether he sleeps or not these days. And we don’t spend much time trying to get him to sleep. Our problem is not trying to get him to sleep. Our problem is that he just falls asleep. Wherever, whenever. It doesn’t need to be flat or dark now. And carrying a hot, sleepy thing is even harder if you don’t have a buggy.

Sleep is the new battle ground. Have you noticed how much bragging there is about it these days? CEO’s being interviewed and saying, Oh, I get up at 5am, work out for an hour before my macrobiotic paleo breakfast before I hit the office at 7am for a 14 hour shift. Recently, 4am appears to be the new 5am. Start up CEOs need an extra hour. Not an extra hour to sleep. No, an extra hour to work. From my own experience and seeing the effect it has on my kids, I’m going the other way. Sleep is more important than just about anything else. I know it takes up time. Time you could be spending watching more TV, posting more of those selfies and checking up on your ‘friends’ online. But, hey, what about going to bed?

I am getting about 8-9 hours sleep every night at the moment. And it's bliss. I feel great most days. (OK, so I don’t have work to do. No commute. Very little stress. No internet, so nothing to waste my time on and 3 staff at our villa here in Bali to do the cooking, cleaning and housework…to be honest If I didn’t sleep, then I’m not sure what I’d do).

But it's very obvious when our children don’t get enough sleep. The day afterwards, their attention is limited, they are irritable and prone to frustration. Most people must be like this every day. People like surgeons, pilots, politicians and soldiers. People whose judgment is pretty important. People who you'd want to sleep more.

We need more sleep-monster role models. I am happy to put myself up for nomination. But I would love to read about the first big company CEO who comes out openly and states that he needs 8 hours a night otherwise he is letting down his shareholders (because he probably is).

Let's not celebrate CEOs who come out about their sexuality, let's honour those who come out, loud and proud and say they really love a nap.
