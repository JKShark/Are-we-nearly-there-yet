Surf's up

You know nothing. In fact you know less than nothing. If you knew you knew nothing, that would be something. but you don’t.

Everything I know about surfing comes from Point Break. I assume that it's an accurate non-fiction documentary, right? Well, now I've actually been surfing on Bondi Beach. So I’m now a dude. A warrior. A punk.

Well, when I say surfing, I mean body boarding. Kind of baby surfing…

...on my daughter’s pink body board.

So there’s a chance that I’m not yet a dude.

But I plan to be. I have a surf lesson booked and I'm gonna catch me some waves.

I thought it best to write down some thoughts about surfing before reality kicks in and I realise I'm far too old to learn to surf. It has such romance. Surfing. Not just a sport but a search for spirituality, the perfect wave and lifelong bonds with fellow kindred spirits.

I first saw surfers in Bali. They would meet us on the beach after work to spend a couple of hours surfing before heading to the bar (after their work day, you understand, we don't do that these days). We were just down there to watch the sunset.

They would warm up on the beach (wetsuits in Sydney, not needed in Bali) and I couldn't help thinking that's not right. Even though it's hard work and it's a sport, warming up goes against the grain. I'm a free spirit. I'm searching for the source. I don't need to swing my arms and stretch my hamstrings.

Anyway. Then there is what looks like the tough / boring bit. Paddling out to sea through the waves. Before you get to sit, look cool and wait for the One.

The wave you're going to ride. And from my limited time spent watching people do this, about 10% of the people sat out there on their boards can actually surf. I suspect that's 'cos it's really hard.

But those that can, really do make it look easy. And cool. And fun.

I know I'm going to be in the 90% who can't ride a wave. To be honest I'm a bit worried that I won't be able to do the bit on the beach where you practice standing still on a board. On the sand. But I can't wait.

We're going to Hawaii and California still on this trip. So if I manage to learn the basics, I have lots of opportunity to practice, with the sharks.

We saw a movie on Great White sharks in Hong Kong which was trying to tell us they weren't too scary. But I wasn't convinced. They looked pretty scary to me. And their favourite spots? South East Australia, Hawaii and California. They are following me round the world. Have they seen my itinerary? And they love those white boards hanging on the ceiling with legs dangling down.

I'm going to play the odds. So much ocean. So many surfers. The chances of being attacked are tiny.

Surfing's the source man, swear to God.
