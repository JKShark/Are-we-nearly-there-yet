Time to share

Life's no fun without someone to share it with. Though I can't be the only one to think that the sharing culture is getting a bit out of hand. Of course I'm a hypocrite. I've been blogging and instagramming and tweeting my way around the world.

It's selfies I object to. The trend for selfies in Asia is bonkers. At our hotel in Thailand, several of the couples (no kids of course) spent a whole day taking pictures of themselves. And I mean literally the whole day. Eight hours of posing and outfit changes and tripods and no doubt hundreds of photos of...themselves. Not the hotel or the scenery. Just the two of them gurning and giving the V sign and jumping up in the air.

And then last night (our last in Bali), two couples spent all night taking pictures of each other, themselves and posing, completely ignoring the beauty of the spot. The waves. The sunset. Anything. It was pure egocentric behaviour. Like babies before they're aware that there are other people in the world. It cannot be a positive for the human race to be regressing to childhood like this.

And, while I'm at it, what is it with staring at your smartphone on the streets, while walking. This happens all the time. Wandering around Asia, we've been confronted with the tops of people's heads. Illuminated from below by a glowing screen. People are literally bumping into lamp posts and bins and fellow citizens. Now I love my iPhone as much as the next man but give it up already. Stand still. Look at your phone. Tippy tap a message. And then put it away and go where you need to go. You cannot do both.

All this sharing did, however, get me thinking about this trip. How would it be different if I was on my own? More sleep? More drinking (if that's possible)? More time to think?

But definitely less fun. Less laughter.

A big part of the experience, any experience, is being able to share it. And I don't mean like this on the blog. And I don't mean sending selfies out into the ether. I mean sharing it live. With real people. People right next to you. People you love.

Talking about this trip as we travel around and reminiscing about it when we return, is part of the fun. Almost part of the trip itself. And being able to do that with Helen and the kids makes it even better than the real thing. Even more real somehow.

This does mean of course that unless my close friends have all read this blog and are fully up to date when we get back, then we're going to bore you for months if not years about this. And just wait till I've got my photo slideshow set up (we've got 1,000 photos of China alone). The evenings will fly by!!
