Trapped in paradise

A romantic island hideaway. Picture perfect for most. Whets your appetite. Indulges your dreams. Dares you to stay. Perched on the cliffs overlooking the ocean. Home. But for how long?

You can live on Hayman Island. There are two private residences here and a school. Families live here permanently. The initial reaction is wow, what an amazing place to live. On the cliffs. Sunshine almost every day. Sailing. Diving. Swimming. Bliss. Perfect for a week. Two. Even a month. But for how much longer?

You start thinking of the realities. No shops. No grocer. No friends. No theatre. No cinema. Only four places to eat at, every single day. How long before paradise becomes a prison? How long before the amazing wildlife and gorgeous views are ignored?

Not too long I fear. For the staff who work here, it is a similar experience though with worse food and worse accommodation. I'd like to think that the opportunity for a spell in paradise and so many activities compensates. For most, according to the ones I've spoken to, it does. Most of the staff are here on a 6 month tour and most feel that is the right amount of time. Which means a lot of staff turnover. Which must be a real pain if you’re trying to run a 5 star hotel where continuity and standards are vital.

It shows too. I don't want to sound curmudgeonly but the levels of service and food quality are not 5 star. Everyone is lovely. And nothing could detract from our stay here (which is what most people probably feel). And for us, this doesn't have to be perfect. Just a lovely place to stay before heading to the wastelands of New Zealand (according to any Aussies who I tell where we're going next). So my expectation of the place doesn't have to colour the reality.

But if you stay here, then get used to asking for things. A lot. Make sure you order two of everything. They always forget one. Don't sit there thinking that the last main course is on its way out. It's not. They've forgotten it. Don't expect to get served at the bar. Ever. If you book some transport around the island, it won't be there. You need to book it again. And I believe it must be clean your own room this week too.

Do, however, expect to find hungover staff. Drunk staff if you're lucky. Naked swimming drunk staff, the jackpot.

And look, I know how lucky I am to even be here. But I'm here to provide a public service. I'm here so you don't have to be. And I would come back again in a heartbeat. But I wouldn't stay at the hotel. I would sail around here (as indeed most people do) and drop anchor, admire the views and maybe pop in for lunch.

So the house hunt continues. Island paradise is not for me. Not long term. Unless of course the island is Australia, or maybe, even, the UK?
