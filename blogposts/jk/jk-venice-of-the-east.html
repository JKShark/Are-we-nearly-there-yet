The Venice of the East

Can you make a comparison if you've never seen something with your own eyes? Can you say it's as cold as the North Pole if you've never been there? Is that still valid? Can you say it's as wet as Manchester if you haven't experienced the numbing depression of opening the curtains every morning to see the grey sodden clouds squeezing out their tears?

We visited Suzhou, to the west of Shanghai in appalling weather. An hour and a half in the car during which it didn't stop raining for a minute. Suzhou is an ancient city that is often called the Venice of the East.

I've no idea why.

I've been to Venice. I know what it's like. I have seen its beauty. And I feel well qualified to use Venice as a comparator if I need to. Suzhou is no Venice.

Yes there are canals in both cities. And...to be honest, that's all they have in common.

Venice is tiny with 270,000 people living there. Suzhou is enormous with over 4 million people and the population growing at the fastest rate in China. Venice is all canals, with over 400 bridges to allow people to walk around. Suzhou is the busiest inland river port in the world.

To be fair, Suzhou does have some amazing gardens, which are collectively a UNESCO world heritage site. Though we didn’t visit them. I’ve no idea why. Too distracted by the container ships passing gently down the ‘canals’ to the port, I’d guess.

If Suzhou is the Venice of the East, then Manchester is the Venice of the North.
