We are sailing

La La Llama. The gale force winds had died down. The sun was still shining. So we hit the harbour.

Some old friends from London, the Lintons, who now live in Sydney own a boat. I know nothing about boats. Have rarely even been on one. So I promise not to get involved. I’m not even sure what to call it. It was a boat with a sail - does that make it a yacht? I did learn that a boat without a sail and only engines to power it is called a Stinkboat. I like that.

What I am good at is sitting, chatting, drinking and eating. My role for the day is thus defined. I fulfil expectations perfectly.

Turns out the boat thing we were on is pretty easy to drive (do you drive a boat? Skipper it? Sail it?). I’ve done a bit of flying (as in, being a pilot) but a boat is easy by comparison. No extensive pre-flight checklist. Untie the ship thing. Start the put-put motor and get off the berth. A few minutes later (still no wind) and we are entering Sydney harbour - just like that. No permission needed. No license. We are there. Sailing towards the opera house and the bridge, dodging the odd ferry and paddle boarder (it’s an eclectic mix in the harbour).

There seem to be some vague rules about rights of way but not much. We can go where we like. Freedom. It feels amazing. Just don’t hit anything. Even my two year old drives it for a bit. Easy peasy. I’ve never loved sailing before but now I get it.

We sailed right past the Opera House and right under the most famous bridge in the world. Then found a quiet bay to drop anchor and sat in the sun on the boat with a picnic. This was my Saturday afternoon but I'm on holiday so every day is the weekend for me. What was dawning on me was that they do this quite a lot, the locals. Lunch in the harbour. On the boat. A bit of swimming. Afternoon kip and then back home for a barbie.

Hard to get used to a routine like that...

On the way back the wind got up a bit. At least enough for us to put the sail up and turn the engine off. And it's quiet. Really quiet. Just the sound of the sun crisping your skin and the wind talking you home. Not too much tacking, gybing and hoisting (I've no idea what any of those mean) and we're following the sun back home.

Saturday afternoons in Sydney. They're rubbish.
