Vietnam

We landed in Hanoi, the capital city of Vietnam. Hanoi is really busy. There are thousands of scooters everywhere. A lot more than cars. You have to walk on the roads because so many scooters are parked on the pavements. To cross the roads, you have to walk slowly. You have to be calm, careful and brave. You don’t wait for the lights or anything because no one stops at the lights.

Most people are buddhists. They worship in pagodas and temples and they like to burn incense sticks. They sing their prayers. They go to the pagoda on the 1st and 15th of every lunar month.

In the pagodas, you see lots of statues and lots of offerings on the altar. Biscuits and money and oranges. They have 4 sacred animals: the turtle which represents long life and hard work; the unicorn which represents protection; the dragon which represents power and strength; and the phoenix which represents prosperity. The unicorns guard the pagoda, the turtle is inside and the dragons are on the roof.

In Hanoi, we went to Ho Chi Minh’s mausoleum and we saw his real dead body. We had to be really quiet as a token of respect. I needed to sneeze when walking through his tomb but I couldn’t because you had to be really quiet. There were four guards inside with guns and swords therefore I had to hold my breath for the rest of the walk.

We went on a rickshaw ride around the city. We saw lots of mobile shops which are scooters with all of the goods strapped onto them. We saw one with thousands of bouquets of flowers. Another with enough hats for everyone at Prospect House hanging off it from all angles.
