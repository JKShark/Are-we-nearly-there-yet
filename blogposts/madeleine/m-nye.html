Bang, Crash, Boom

On New Year’s Eve it was all very exciting. We to a big party on the beach where food was served on ice sculptures.

There was a huge stage where people were singing very loudly. It was so loud that I thought people could hear it all the way back in London. I went on the dancefloor with Mummy and we danced crazily amongst the huge mass of people.

There were thousands and millions of people at the party. Lots of people were very dressed up and I saw two really posh ladies in really decorated ball gowns. They looked really beautiful, like princesses.

Freddie was really crazy at the beginning but then slept for most of the party. Alex, Freddie and I all ran around loads, exploring all the food stations. One of the ice sculptures near our table provided lots of entertainment. Freddie ran up to it and started licking it as if it was a lollipop.

My favourite food station was the puddings, which included cake pops hanging from a tree, a palm tree made from dates, a chocolate pyramid covered in strawberries and a macaroon tower. We got gob-stoppers from the kids’ zone, which were huge for London, but mini for Dubai!

Near the end we were very tired, but fortunately Mummy made beds out of chairs for us to lie on. Finally the fireworks started at midnight. They were amazing! They spiralled up and whizzed down. Up! Down! Bang! Crash! Fireworks were exploding all around us, in flashes of red, yellow, pink, purple, all the colours of the rainbow.

At last it was bedtime and not a moment too soon. It was 1am!
